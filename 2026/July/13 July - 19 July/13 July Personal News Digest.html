Former County Executive McClure Loses PA-7 Primary, Now Backs Winner Bob Brooks

Why it matters: Northampton County is a core part of the 7th Congressional District, so the outcome of this primary and the fall matchup directly determines who represents county residents in Washington and shapes the county's political leadership pipeline after McClure's exit from local office.

Bob Brooks won the Democratic nomination for Pennsylvania's 7th Congressional District, defeating former Northampton County Executive Lamont McClure along with Ryan Crosswell and Carol Obando-Derstine; Brooks will face Republican incumbent Ryan Mackenzie in November. County Council Passes McClure's Final Budget, an Eighth Straight Year With No Tax Hike

Why it matters: This budget sets the property tax rate and service levels every Northampton County household and business will live under for the year, and it marks a fiscal baseline the incoming County Executive administration inherits after McClure's departure.

Detail & sources

Following County Council approval, Executive Lamont McClure announced the county passed its eighth consecutive budget without a property tax increase, keeping the rate at 10.8 mills, a streak officials say is unmatched in county records dating to at least 1961. The $503 million 2026 spending plan cuts spending by 7.3%, uses about $5 million from the general fund to balance, and preserves McClure's core commitments on open space, Gracedale nursing home, and no tax hikes.
