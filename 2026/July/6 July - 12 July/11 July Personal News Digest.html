Federal Report Finds Fire Inspections Lapsed for Years Before Easton's Hotel Hampton Blaze

Why it matters: Matters to residents because it exposes gaps in code enforcement that a city official says let a known boarding house operate for years without required fire inspections, raising questions about whether the same oversight failures could put other Northampton County renters at risk.

Detail & sources

A newly completed federal investigation into the Hotel Hampton fire found the Downtown Easton boarding house had not undergone fire inspections since at least 2017 despite a history of code violations and prior safety concerns; the blaze seriously injured a firefighter and left dozens homeless. City leaders are now being pressed on the oversight failure, with Easton Planning and Codes Director Dwayne Tillman telling officials that records dating to a 1977 certificate of occupancy consistently identified the property as a hotel rather than a rooming or boarding house, a distinction tied to which safety rules applied.

Iconic Dixie Cup Factory Comes Down as 400-Unit Apartment Project Advances

Why it matters: Matters to county taxpayers and Easton-area residents because it marks the most visible progress yet on redeveloping a long-vacant industrial landmark into housing, even as questions persist over political influence on the county's tax-increment financing vote that helped clear the way.

Detail & sources

Crews are dismantling parts of the historic Dixie Cup structure as Skyline Investment Group moves forward with plans to convert the long-vacant factory site into more than 400 apartments, restoring iconic elements of the building. The project remains linked to controversy after a Pennsylvania 7th Congressional District candidate had to refute claims that he improperly influenced Northampton County's TIF vote for the Dixie Cup site.
