Easton officials pressed on code-enforcement failures tied to Hotel Hampton fire

Why it matters: Residents of Easton's rental housing stock need assurance that city inspectors are correctly classifying and monitoring boarding-style properties, after officials acknowledged the Hotel Hampton had been on record as a hotel — not a rooming house — dating back decades, raising questions about whether proper fire-safety oversight was applied.

Detail & sources

Easton's Planning and Codes Director, Dwayne Tillman, told officials that city records dating to a 1977 certificate of occupancy consistently identified the Hotel Hampton property as a hotel rather than a rooming or boarding house, a classification distinction that affects which fire and safety codes apply. The disclosure came amid continued scrutiny of the city's oversight of the property following a fatal fire there, with local reporting pressing city leaders on whether inspection and code-enforcement protocols were adequate.
